RESOURCES

 Guides and Tips

  • If someone goes missing, report to the nearest barangay or police station immediately.
  • Have a recent photo, physical description, and last known location ready when reporting.
  • Always keep calm and coordinate with official responders.
  • Don’t wait 24 hours — report as soon as you notice someone is missing.

Legal and Government Help

  • Local government units (LGUs), like your barangay or city hall, can assist in filing official reports and mobilizing community support.
  • The Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) provides help for vulnerable groups, including children, elderly, and persons with disabilities.
  • Police authorities can issue blotter reports, conduct search operations, and work with national agencies when needed.
  • Government offices can also help you access legal aid, transport, or temporary shelter if necessary.

Emergency Hotlines

 Awareness and Advice

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and report any suspicious activities.
  • Educate your community on what to do during missing person incidents.

 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Q: How long do I need to wait before reporting? A: You don’t have to wait — report immediately.
  • Q: Can I report someone with mental illness or Alzheimer's? A: Yes. Early reporting is encouraged for all vulnerable individuals.
  • Q: Can I stay anonymous when reporting? A: You can request confidentiality, but responders may still need basic details for verification.
